Clint Eastwood Biography, Age, Height, Wife, Net Worth, Family

Age, Biography and Wiki

Clint Eastwood is an American actor, filmmaker, musician, and political figure. He has been in the entertainment industry for over six decades and has won four Academy Awards, four Golden Globe Awards, and three Screen Actors Guild Awards. He is also the recipient of the AFI Life Achievement Award. Eastwood was born on May 31, 1930, in San Francisco, California. He is the son of Clinton Eastwood Sr., a steelworker and migrant worker, and Margaret Ruth (Runner) Eastwood, a housewife. He has two older siblings, an older sister, Jeanne Bernhardt, and a younger brother, Hal Eastwood. Eastwood attended Piedmont Middle School and Oakland Technical High School. He then attended Los Angeles City College, where he studied drama. He also served in the United States Army from 1951 to 1953. Eastwood began his acting career in 1954, appearing in the television series Rawhide. He went on to star in a number of films, including A Fistful of Dollars (1964), The Good, the Bad and the Ugly (1966), Dirty Harry (1971), and Unforgiven (1992). He has also directed and produced a number of films, including Play Misty for Me (1971), The Outlaw Josey Wales (1976), and Million Dollar Baby (2004). Eastwood has been married twice. He was married to Maggie Johnson from 1953 to 1984, and to Dina Ruiz from 1996 to 2014. He has seven children. As of 2021, Clint Eastwood's net worth is estimated to be $375 million.
