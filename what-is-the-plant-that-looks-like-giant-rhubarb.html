These biennial plants produce large, heart-shaped wavy leaves and tall reddish-colored stalks similar to rhubarb in USDA plant hardiness zones 2 through 10.Additionally, what is the difference between rhubarb and burdock? The mature leaves of Burdock are less curly than rhubarb, and the undersides of the leaves are “wooly” or “hairy”, unlike rhubarb. The stalks of Burdock are hollow, while the stalks of rhubarb are solid. Burdock plants (weeds) can grow to a height of 6 feet, unlike rhubarb plants. Furthermore, can you eat giant rhubarb? Although Giant rhubarb is not poisonous to humans, the leaf blade and stem bear stiff bristles which can scratch skin. Giant rhubarb resembles very large common garden rhubarb.Are there weeds that look like rhubarb?Wild rhubarb is also known as common burdock and it is commonly mistaken for rhubarb because of its appearance.
