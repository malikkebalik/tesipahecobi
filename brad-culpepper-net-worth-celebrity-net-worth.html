Brad Culpepper net worth and salary: Brad Culpepper is an American former professional football player who has a net worth of $3 million dollars. Brad Culpepper was born in Nashville, Tennessee on May 8, 1969. He grew up in Tallahassee, Florida. He followed in the footsteps of his family and went on to attend the University of Florida at Gainesville on an athletic scholarship. Brad's father and uncle both played on the schools football team, and his grandfather was an alumni and one time chancellor of the state university school system in Florida. During his junior year, the Gators won the SEC Championship. Brad won the Draddy Trophy, the award given to the best college student-athlete in the US. He graduated after his junior year with a degree in History. The Minnesota Vikings drafted him in 1992, and he played for the team from 1992-1993. Culpepper then played for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers from 1994-1999. He spent his final season, 2000, with the Chicago Bears. After retiring from professional football, Brad returned to school to complete his Master's Degree.

He also earned a law degree. He is now a trial lawyer in Tampa, Florida and a contestant on Survivor. During his NFL career, Brad Culpepper earned a cumulative $4.9 million in salary. He is 6 feet 1 inches tall and currently weighs around 180 pounds. At the peak of his NFL career, Brad's weight topped 280 pound, which he has since admitted was unnatural and unsafe. In the 1992 draft, Brad was the 264th pick in the 10th round. He was inducted into the University of Florida Athletic Hall of Fame in 2001 and is often pointed to as one of the best examples of a student athlete any college has ever produced. Brad and his wife Monica finished 15th on Suvivor: Blood Vs. Water, and were so popular that thousands of audience members have demanded they be brought back onto the show in some capacity.
