Meet Michael Jackson Lookalike Fabio Jackson 

Fabio Jackson (age: 30, June 13, 1993) is a lookalike of the King of Pop, Michael Jackson. Fabio has become an influential figure on social media platforms, captivating audiences worldwide with his uncanny resemblance to the pop icon Michael Jackson.

Due to his resemblance to the pop star, Fabio was in a few documentaries built around Michael Jackson, including “Fatal Addiction: Michael Jackson” and “The Last 24 Hours: Michael Jackson,” which came out in 2018.

Despite facing criticism and accusations, Fabio has remained resilient and continued to build a successful career as a Michael Jackson lookalike and influencer. This article will provide an in-depth look into Fabio’s life, shedding light on his experiences and achievements.

How Old is Fabio Jackson?

Fabio Jackson (age 30) was born on June 13, 1993, in Norfolk, England. Growing up, he spent his entire childhood in the picturesque town of Great Yarmouth.

While Fabio rarely discusses his parents and siblings, it is known that he had a challenging time during his school days due to his striking resemblance to Michael Jackson.

Throughout his schooling, Fabio faced constant teasing and bullying from his classmates. His peers found his resemblance to the pop icon both intriguing and bewildering, leading to a difficult period in his life.

In 2011 he completed schooling and joined UCS (University Campus Suffolk) to earn a degree in multimedia.

Fabio Jackson’s Career 

According to sources, Fabio came to London in 2016 after completing college. In London, he began looking for acting opportunities. Luckily due to his look of Michael Jackson, he did not face any difficulty and soon got to be part of a documentary titled “The Last 24 Hours: Michael Jackson.”

Meanwhile, he also started establishing his presence on social media platforms. His dance videos and short clips quickly got viral on TikTok, earning him thousands of followers. Soon from thousands, the count of his followers reached millions. Currently, over 11 million people follow him on TikTok.

Eventually, he created a YouTube channel to upload random videos. In 2022 he appeared in “Fatal Addiction: Michael Jackson,” a documentary covering Michael Jackson’s addiction to painkillers.
